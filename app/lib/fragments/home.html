Esdar is an innovation-driven education initiative delivering practical Robotics & AI learning to students. We partner with schools end-to-end — lab setup, camps, kits, trained instructors, and curriculum — so students learn by building, not just reading.

What we do

Everything a modern robotics & AI program needs — from one partner

We specialize in engaging, hands-on learning that lets students explore technology beyond textbooks. From the lab to the curriculum to the instructors, Esdar handles the whole journey.

Lab setup

Complete robotics, AI, and VR/AR learning environments — interior design, equipment, and all the kits, installed and ready.

Camps

High-impact 7-day, 15-day, and 3-month camps where students build real projects in robotics, AI, and drones.

Curriculum & kits

A structured, grade-wise learning path with workbooks, plus all the Arduino, Micro:bit, and drone kits students need.

Trained instructors

Certified, full-time technical trainers who deliver engaging, hands-on sessions alongside your staff.

Competitions & tech fest

We prepare students for competitions and host an annual tech fest and exhibitions that showcase their work.

Projects & outcomes

Students ship real, demonstrable projects — from line-following robots to AI object-sorting arms.

Our mission

Learning by doing, not by watching

Our approach emphasizes learning by doing, where students actively build, experiment, and understand concepts in a structured and guided environment.

Through our programs and workshops, we nurture curiosity, creativity, and problem-solving skills to prepare students for future technology opportunities.

Student work · Projects

Real projects, really built

A glimpse at what Esdar students actually build — from a Primary-level flashing-heart Micro:bit to a Senior-level AI object-sorting arm. The full grade-wise list lives on the Projects page.

Primary (Grades 1–5)

Flashing Heart

A simple animation of a beating heart on the Micro:bit LED matrix.

Primary (Grades 1–5)

Gesture-Controlled Robot

Sensors detect hand gestures; the robot moves in different directions based on input.

Primary (Grades 1–5)

Face ID Door Unlock

A webcam recognises faces; the door unlocks automatically for authorised users.

Intermediate (Grades 6–8)

Line Following Robot

A 2-wheel-drive chassis using IR sensors to track a black line.

Senior (Grades 9–10)

IoT Weather Dashboard

Send temperature and humidity data to a cloud platform like ThingSpeak.

Senior (Grades 9–10)

AI Object Sorting Arm

A neural network recognises recyclable vs organic waste; a robotic arm sorts accordingly.
