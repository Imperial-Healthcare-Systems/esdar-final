Why it works

Some things are better experienced than explained

Augmented and virtual reality let students see the invisible and visit the impossible. When a concept becomes something you can move around and interact with, understanding stops being memorisation and starts being real.

Remember more

Immersive, hands-on experiences stick far longer than passive reading — students recall what they lived, not just what they read.

Go anywhere

From the surface of Mars to inside the human body to historic landmarks — the whole world becomes a classroom.
